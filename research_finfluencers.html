Finfluencer Impact Study

Understanding how social‑media influencers reshape retail investor psychology and market dynamics.

Project Overview

During my internship under Professor Ania Kapalczynski at the University of Puget Sound, I set out to examine how different social‑media platforms affected investment behavior. When preliminary data defied my hypothesis, I pivoted to isolate the true catalyst: a growing wave of finfluencers disseminating identical narratives across every channel.

This page distills the journey—from framing the research question to iterative methodology shifts—and showcases the ultimate insights that emerged.

Key Contributions

  • Built a 100k‑tweet dataset using Python & Twitter API, tagging sentiment and source credibility.
  • Applied topic‑modeling (LDA) to cluster influencer narratives, uncovering message homogenization over time.
  • Demonstrated a 27 % increase in retail order‑flow volatility during synchronized finfluencer “pump” cycles.
  • Co‑authored a forthcoming conference abstract; slides available below.
